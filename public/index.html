Greetings Fellow Rubyists!

My name is Daniel M Martin, my friends call me Dan. I live in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada, where I have spent a good portion of my life.

Occupation

I am a medical doctor, practicing hospital care at a local community hospital.

Sometimes called a "hospitalist" or HMO (House Medical Officer, which is not completely accurate), my current job is to look after the medical needs of patients on an orthopedic ward.

Computer Programming

I started taking computer science in high school in the 1970s, programming on punch cards which were physically taken to a university mainframe in a different part of the city. Later, we switched to a different government mainframe with a punch card / modem hookup.

I was hooked, and continued to program in my spare time. There was not a lot of spare time in medical school nor in clinical practice. While in residency, I took out a loan to buy my first computer, a Commodore 64.

In the late 1990s I worked as an assistant in vascular surgery and attended the University of Manitoba taking Computer Science. I finally took a year off from work to complete my degree, an (Honours) Bachelor of Computer Science.

In 2000 I started work as a hospitalist, which meant I was extremely busy during my rotations but then had blocks of time off. From 2000 to 2007 I worked on my MSc. It took a long time, scheduling courses between rotations. I finally settled on a thesis topic, involving taking arithmetic methods of CT reconstruction and running them in parallel on a simulated CT scanner.

Since then I have dabbled, writing programs for my own use including a medical billings program.

Some day I would like to write a program and acutually get paid for it.

Ruby

I learned to program in Waterloo Fortran, and it was easy to learn Basic for my C64. In university I was taught Pascal, and exposed to Lisp, Algol, and Java. I taught myself C and C++ and used C++ for many years. I am currently learning Objective C and Cocoa on my Mac. I used Perl to write part of my billings program, then, a couple years ago, stumbled upon Ruby.

I was looking for something to do web programming and of course found many references to Rails. I never did get Rails working for me, but I was enthralled by the expressiveness and sheer beauty of Ruby. I now use Ruby for almost everything.

Course: Programming for the Web with Ruby

After my next work rotation I will be taking the course Programming for the Web with Ruby, presented by Satish Talim.

I hope to learn about presenting dynamic web pages using Ruby, and also how to use web-like interfaces in other Ruby applications.
